### Listening and Active Communication
#### Question 1: What are the steps or strategies to practice Active Listening?

Active listening means giving full attention to the speaker. The main strategies are:
* Avoid distractions and focus on the conversation.
* Do not interrupt; let the speaker finish speaking.
* Use simple phrases like “Tell me more” or “I understand” to show interest.
* Use positive body language such as eye contact and nodding.
* Take notes when the conversation is important.
* Repeat the main points in your own words to confirm understanding.  

#### Question 2: According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

The key points of reflective listening are:
* Mirroring the speaker’s emotions and body language to show understanding.
* Verifying the message by restating it in simple words.
* Validating the speaker so they feel heard and understood.

#### Question 3: What are the obstacles in your listening process?

Some common obstacles in listening are:
* Internal distractions, like thinking about personal issues or planning a reply.
* External distractions, such as noise or phone notifications.
* Making assumptions before the speaker finishes speaking.
* Emotional reactions like anger or defensiveness.

#### Question 4: What can you do to improve your listening?

Listening skills can be improved by:
* Staying focused and being present during conversations.
* Using active listening techniques like paraphrasing and encouraging words.
* Paying attention to body language and non-verbal cues.
* Noticing distractions or emotions and correcting them.

#### Question 5: When do you switch to a passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

A passive communication style may be used when:
* Trying to avoid conflict or arguments.
* Feeling nervous or uncomfortable around certain people.
* Putting others’ needs before your own.

#### Question 6: When do you switch to aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

Aggressive communication may happen when:
* Feeling angry or stressed for a long time.
* Personal needs are ignored repeatedly.
* Trying to control a situation without considering others.

#### Question 7: When do you switch to passive-aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

Passive-aggressive communication may be used when:
* Feeling angry but unable to express it directly.
* Using sarcasm, silence, or indirect comments.
* Holding in feelings and not speaking openly. 

#### Question 8: How can you make your communication assertive?

Communication can be more assertive by:
* Clearly expressing your feelings and needs.
* Knowing what you want to say before speaking.
* Practicing assertiveness in simple situations.
* Matching body language with words.
* Speaking up early to avoid misunderstandings.
